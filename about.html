How I Work

A few things I believe about good systems.

🎯

Simple beats clever.

A system your team understands and can maintain is worth more than an elegant one only I can modify. I build for handoff, not dependency.

📐

Diagnose before you build.

Most workflow problems aren't what they look like on the surface. I spend time understanding the real constraint before touching any tools or code.

🔒

You own the outcome.

Every engagement ends with something you control — documented, trained, and fully in your hands. No lock-in to me or any particular platform.

📏

Right-size the solution.

A 10-person team and a 200-person team need different things. I don't over-engineer for scale you don't have, or under-build for growth that's coming.

📝

Document the why, not just the what.

A process without explanation is a fragile process. I document the reasoning behind decisions so future changes don't require archaeology.

🗣️

Plain language, always.

I don't hide behind technical jargon. If I can't explain what I'm building and why in plain English, I'm probably building the wrong thing.
